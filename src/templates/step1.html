Step 1: Assign initial characteristic scores

The characteristics listed in the left column are created by rolling 3d6 and represent what you'd imagine them to:

  • STR: A character's strength. This characteristic is a factor in calculating hand-to-hand combat damage. If a character's strength is reduced to 0, the character becomes an invalid.
  • CON: Constitution is a measure of health, vigor, or vitality. Constitution is used to calculate hit points and certain resistances. If constitution is reduced to 0, a character dies.
  • POW: Power indicates force of will and a character's aptitude for magic. It's used to calculate magic points, resistance to magical or hypnotic attacks, Luck, and Sanity.
  • DEX: Dexterity represents quickness and flexibility. Dexterity is used to determine combat order and the starting percentage of an investigator's Dodge skill.
  • APP: Appearance indicates attractiveness or friendliness. It's generally a characteristic used in conjunction with skills like Fast Talk or Bargain.

Dice rolls are provided in the right column (in the green circles). Click one, and then click the gray circle next to the characteristic you'd like to assign it to. If you're unhappy with the rolls, click the "Reroll" button below. When you're satisfied, click the "Next" button.
