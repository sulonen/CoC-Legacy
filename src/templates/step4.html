Step 4: Derived Scores and Characteristic Rolls

As you can see, your rolls have contributed to some additional scores:

  • SAN: Sanity is POW multiplied by 5.
  • Idea: Refers to hunches and the ability to interpret obvious things.
  • Luck: Represents an investigator's ability to be in the right place at the right time.
  • Know: Is a chance to have knowledge on a subject even without a corresponding skill.
  • Damage Bonus: Additional damage (or penalty) applied to damage rolls in melee combat.
  • 99 - Mythos: This is an odd number and is peculiar to this game system. As an investigator encounters the horrors of the Cthulhu Mythos, their percentile in the Cthulhu Mythos skill increases. This can be both good and bad. The investigator's ability to identify entities and situations (and, hopefully, to counter or evade them) increases. At the same time, exposure to the Mythos tends to whittle away at human sanity. This number represents an investigator's maximum sanity.
  • Hit Points: Are a representation of an investigator's physical health. When hit points are reduced to 2 or less, an investigator becomes unconscious. At 0 or lower, the investigator dies, unless their hit points can be raised to at least +1 by the end of the following round.
  • Magic Points: Magic points are currency in the casting of spells or fighting off malign influences.
  • Sanity Points: As a session progresses, Sanity Points will fluctuate. The SAN characteristic doesn't. Sanity Points are the gauge of an investigator's mental health over time.
